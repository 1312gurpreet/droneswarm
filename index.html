Bridging Graph Neural Network and Reinforcement Learning for Autonomous Drone Swarm Exploration

2021 CUSP Capstone Project - NYU

Autonomous drone swarms can play a critical role in supporting rescue teams in post-disaster by detecting victims, assessing the damage of buildings, and monitoring terrains, especially for inaccessible or dangerous environments for humans. The critical requirement for this application is decentralized control without relying on the Global Positioning System (GPS).

We hypothesize that graph neural network (GNN) as part of the reinforcement learning (RL) policy network would outperform the existing RL approaches in efficiency and scalability. GNN enables us to model drones’ information exchange with nearby ones. Thus, drones can optimize their actions based on richer information to pursue a common global goal. This research focuses on the mapping task; drones try to visit points of interest within the disaster area as fast and thoroughly as possible.

The objectives of this research are 1) to define a baseline performance with the multi-agent Q-learning algorithm that GNN ultimately should outperform and 2) to formulate a concrete problem statement for the GNN algorithm for future work.
